# Voice and Tone

Zeffy's writing voice is **confident, warm, and concrete**. Read like a peer operator at a nonprofit, not a SaaS salesperson.

## Core principles

1. **Lead with the outcome, not the feature.** "Raise more, lose nothing to fees" beats "platform with zero transaction fees."
2. **Use small, honest numbers.** "Save $2,400/year" beats "Save thousands."
3. **Prefer action verbs to adjectives.** "Start fundraising" beats "best-in-class fundraising."
4. **Say "free" plainly.** It's Zeffy's defining fact. Don't hedge with "virtually free," "free tier," or asterisks.
5. **Sound like a nonprofit, not a fund.** Avoid MBA-speak: "stakeholders", "leverage", "solutions" (as a buzzword).
6. **One CTA per view.** "Sign up for free" or "Start fundraising — it's free forever!" are the house CTAs.

## Examples — the Zeffy voice

- "All-in-one fundraising platform with zero fees."
- "Sign up — it's free forever!"
- "From donations and ticketing to raffles, auctions, and donor management. Used and loved by over 100,000 nonprofits."
- "Join 100,000+ nonprofits raising more with zero platform fees."
- "How are we free? Donors are invited to add an optional contribution on top of their gift."

## Anti-patterns — don't write like this

- ❌ "Empower your organization with cutting-edge fundraising solutions." (corporate, vague)
- ❌ "Unlock synergies between donors and operators." (jargon)
- ❌ "Our best-in-class platform delivers enterprise-grade tooling." (feature brag, not outcome)
- ❌ "Low-cost fundraising." (contradicts the free promise)
- ❌ "Click here to learn more!" (weak CTA; always use a verb tied to the outcome)

## Capitalization and punctuation

- **Sentence case** in headings, including buttons. Use title case only for proper product names ("Tap to Pay app").
- **Em-dash** for emphatic asides: "Start fundraising — it's free forever."
- **Straight quotes** for UI copy; smart quotes for editorial / blog copy.
- **No trailing periods** in button labels.
- **The word "free"** is lowercase except at the start of a sentence. "100% free" — never "100% FREE."

## Inclusivity

- Address nonprofit operators, not donors, in product copy. Donors are acknowledged via testimonials and blog content.
- Avoid faith-specific language in house copy; Zeffy serves religious and secular causes equally.
- Use plural / gender-neutral where possible: "your team" not "your guys."

## Voice in error messages

- **Say what happened, then what to do.** "We couldn't save that donation. Check your connection and try again."
- **Never blame the user.** Avoid "You entered an invalid email." Prefer "That doesn't look like a valid email."
